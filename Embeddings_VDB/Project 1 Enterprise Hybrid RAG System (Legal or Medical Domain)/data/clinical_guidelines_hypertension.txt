Hypertension Management Guidelines (2025 Update)

1. Diagnosis:
Hypertension is diagnosed when a patient's resting blood pressure consistently exceeds 130/80 mm Hg on at least two separate clinic visits.
Primary essential hypertension accounts for 95% of cases.

2. Treatment Protocols:
- First-line therapy for non-black patients: ACE inhibitors (e.g., Lisinopril), ARBs (e.g., Losartan), or Calcium Channel Blockers (e.g., Amlodipine).
- First-line therapy for black patients: Thiazide-type diuretics or Calcium Channel Blockers.
- For patients with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD), ACE inhibitors or ARBs are recommended to preserve kidney function regardless of race.

3. Lifestyle Interventions:
- Dietary sodium reduction to <1500 mg/day.
- DASH (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) diet implementation.
- At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic physical activity per week.

4. Follow-up:
Patients starting new antihypertensive therapy should be re-evaluated within 4 weeks. If the blood pressure target is not met, titrate the current medication or add a second agent from a different class.
